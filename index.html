Needs: artists, UI polish, playtesters, sound/music.

Sprout

Chess Book Companion

A chess-learning tool that takes a PGN or recent Lichess game, finds major Stockfish evaluation swings, identifies real patterns from the player’s game, and connects those moments to relevant chess-book ideas.

The goal is not generic AI advice. The goal is a natural bridge between a player’s actual games, their repertoire, and real GM-level chess knowledge.

Possible direction: local/offline LLM, Stockfish, PGN parsing, Lichess import, curated chess-book notes, and transparent source grounding.

First public version

Why this exists

I have too many ideas to keep them hidden in random notes forever. I want this site to be a public garden where ideas can be seen, shaped, challenged, supported, and eventually built with real people.

I use AI as a prototyping and organization tool, but I do not want this to become an AI-sludge machine. I want more artists, educators, builders, gardeners, developers, and human specialists involved — not fewer. This site is a doorway for that.

Working principle

Human-first, AI-transparent

I want to be honest when AI helped with scaffolding, code, wording, or planning. But the direction, taste, care, lived experience, project meaning, and final decisions need to stay human.

Get Involved

I am not trying to build everything alone forever. The point of the garden is to make room for real human collaboration.

Artists

Fish, icons, daisies, groovy UI, project visuals, nature art, game art, and cozy world-building.

Developers

Frontend, Phaser/TypeScript, static site help, hardware sensors, accessibility tools, and lightweight web systems.

Educators & Specialists

EC feedback, accessibility review, plant/nature knowledge, classroom usefulness, and real-world testing.

Collaboration & Use

Clay’s Idea Garden is built for collaboration, not idea hoarding — but it is also not a free-for-all. You are welcome to read, react, share feedback, and reach out if you want to help something grow.

Unless clearly stated otherwise, the project names, writing, prototypes, designs, code, and specific concepts on this site are not being released for unrestricted use. If something sparks an idea, contact me. I would rather build with people than lock everything away.

Support the Garden

Support can mean feedback, sharing, art help, development help, testing, specialist knowledge, encouragement, or future donations. For now, the main goal is to make the garden clearer, more useful, and easier for good people to join.
