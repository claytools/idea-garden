Rogue Fish

Rogue Fish is the first project actively growing in the garden: a fishing/card roguelite with aquarium progression, relics, shops, upgrades, resources, and bright aquatic game energy.

I can keep vibe-coding the prototype, but the project gets much better with real playtesters, artists, UI feedback, sound ideas, and people who can help it feel more polished and alive.

Interesting Sprout

Chess Book Companion

A chess-learning tool that takes a PGN or recent Lichess game, finds major Stockfish evaluation swings, identifies real patterns from the player’s game, and connects those moments to relevant chess-book ideas.

The goal is not generic AI advice. The goal is grounded learning: your actual games, your repertoire, real chess books, clear sources, and useful human-readable lessons.

How this should work

Human-first, AI-transparent, anti-sludge.

I use AI right now to learn, organize, scaffold, and prototype because I am building outside my current skill level. But I do not want these projects to become soulless AI output.

The goal is to move toward more human art, human testing, human craft, human feedback, and human collaboration as the garden grows.

Most help is volunteer or feedback-based for now. I am still figuring out the money model, but I care about clear credit, honest expectations, paying people when funding exists, and making bigger agreements for bigger roles.

Collaboration & Use

Clay’s Idea Garden is built for collaboration, not idea hoarding — but it is also not a free-for-all. You are welcome to read, react, share feedback, and reach out if you want to help something grow.

Unless clearly stated otherwise, project names, writing, prototypes, designs, code, and specific concepts on this site are not being released for unrestricted use. If something sparks an idea, contact me. I would rather build with people than lock everything away.
